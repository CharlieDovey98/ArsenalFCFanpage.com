Our leader through these tough times.Mikel Arteta is a Spanish professional football manager and former player. Born in San Sebastian, Spain in 1982, Arteta joined the Barcelona Youth Academy in 1997 before quickly becoming a regular in the first team. During his time as a footballer, he featured for several top teams in Spain, France, Scotland and England, including Real Sociedad, Paris Saint-Germain, Rangers, Everton and Arsenal.

At Arsenal, Arteta developed a reputation as one of England's premier midfielders, forming a key partnership with compatriot Cesc Fabregas. He also developed good tactical knowledge, which saw him make more than 200 appearances for the club in a four and a half year stay.

In 2016, Arteta retired from professional football after 15 years of playing and was offered a role at Manchester City as part of Pep Guardiola's coaching staff. His time at City saw him learn from one of football's most esteemed managers and helped him develop a strategic and philosophical approach to management.

On 20th December 2019, Arteta took charge of Arsenal after the sacking of Unai Emery, becoming the first Spanish manager to manage in the Premier League. Since then he has combined his tactical nous with a restored discipline and intensity to the side, though results saw a decline for much of his first year in office. However, in his second season, he has helped create an upturn in fortunes at the Emirates Stadium and looks set to return the Gunners to their former glory.

Arsenal is currently at the top of the Premier League table, a few points clear of their closest rivals. They have had an outstanding season so far and have been proving themselves as one of the best teams in England. Their defense in particular has looked solid with the likes of Gabriel, Ben White, and Saliba all contributing to the team's impressive defensive record. Offensively, Odegaard has been on fire this season, creating chances for his teammates and scoring vital goals himself. They have also taken advantage of their summer transfer business having signed Leandro Trossard and Jorginho who have both been impressive, adding goals and looking strong in their defensive duties. Arsenal will be hoping to continue this form and keep their lead at the top of the table throughout the rest of the season.

Latest Results And Upcoming Fixtures

The Arsenal team have been on an impressive run in the current Premier League season. Under manager Mikel Arteta, they have had consistent performances, with their last result being a 1-0 win over Manchester City. Arsenal have continued to show impressive resilience in defence and have managed to keep four clean sheets in the last five games, testament to their newfound ability to concentrate and execute the game-plan.

Going into the next few weeks of the season, Arsenal face some tough matches against Tottenham and Chelsea. These are must-win games for the Gunners if they are to stay in the top half of the table and continue to challenge for a Champions League place. Before then, though, Arsenal have a great chance to close the gap on fourth-placed West Ham when they take on Sheffield United at the Emirates Stadium.

After that, Arsenal travel to Anfield to take on fierce rivals Liverpool. The Reds are currently sitting at the top of the table, making this a difficult test for the Gunners. With the Merseysiders in a commanding position, it’s likely that the game could be all about defensive organisation for the Arsenal team, who will no doubt be looking to counter when possible and limit Liverpool’s attacking play.

The Gunners then host West Bromwich Albion in their next Premier League match. This provides Arsenal with a great opportunity to close the gap on fourth-placed West Ham and put themselves in a strong position to challenge for a return to Champions League football.

Finally, Arsenal finish off their league campaign with a trip to Crystal Palace. Palace have been relatively consistent and reliable this season and will no doubt be looking to finish with a good result at home. Arsenal will be determined to end the season on a high and a victory in this penultimate match of the season could be a huge boost in their bid for Champions League football.

Our Rivals

Tottenham Hotspur FC. Arsenal and Tottenham Hotspur are fierce rivals in the English Premier League, often nicknamed the 'North London Derby.' The two sides have a long and storied history that dates back more than 130 years. For most of the 20th century, Spurs were the more successful of the two teams, having won the league title twice, the FA Cup eight times and the League Cup four times.

In recent years, however, Arsenal have turned the tables and become the bigger club. The Gunners have won the title three times since 1996 and have achieved more Champions League success than their bitter rivals.

The heat of the rivalry has even been known to spill out of the football field and onto the streets of North London on occasion. In 2014, a fight erupted between fans at the Emirates Stadium after Arsenal sealed a 1-0 win. This was preceded by a series of clashes between rival fans in 2013.

Despite the ferocity of the rivalry, the two sides often share a mutual respect and the matches between them are often highly competitive and entertaining. The rivalry is also rooted in the local communities of the two clubs, with supporters of the clubs often living close to each other.

For supporters of Arsenal and Spurs, nothing compares to the excitement and passion of the North London Derby. It is one of the most iconic rivalries in English football and the matches between the two clubs are always eagerly anticipated.
